# Bad Parrot — Japanese Slang Reference

⚠️ **Content not yet validated by native speakers.** Use as a working document for phrase curation.

Sources: Coto Academy, Wasabi Magazine, Scripting Japan, Takashionary, Languagerealm.

---

## Slang Level Classification

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Street** | Casual but standard. だ/る endings, natural conversation between friends. |
| 2 | **Familier** | Colloquial, shortened forms, dropped particles. Very informal. |
| 3 | **Gen Z** | Youth slang, internet-born, abbreviations. Can sound extreme or playful. |

---

## Reactions & Exclamations

| Term | Reading | Meaning | Level | Usage note |
|------|---------|---------|-------|------------|
| やばい | yabai | crazy / awesome / oh no | 1→3 | All-purpose reaction. Context tells if positive or negative. |
| まじ？ | maji? | Really? / No way! | 1 | Standard casual surprise. |
| ガチ？ | gachi? | Seriously? (stronger) | 2 | From sumo ガチンコ. More emphatic than まじ. |
| それな | sore na | Exactly! / Facts! | 2 | Enthusiastic agreement. Kansai origin. |
| うそ！ | uso! | No way! / You're kidding! | 1 | Literally "lie". |
| ぴえん | pien | 😢 (cute sadness) | 3 | Onomatopoeia for tear falling. Playful, not serious. |
| 草 | kusa | LOL | 3 | From "www" (laugh) resembling grass. Text/internet. |

## Intensifiers ("very")

| Term | Reading | Meaning | Level | Usage note |
|------|---------|---------|-------|------------|
| めっちゃ | meccha | really / super | 2 | Kansai origin, now nationwide. |
| めちゃくちゃ | mechakucha | extremely | 2 | Stronger than めっちゃ. |
| 超 | chou | super / so | 2 | Youth slang. 超おいしい = super tasty. |
| マジ | maji | really / seriously | 2 | One of the most common slang intensifiers. |
| ガチ | gachi | seriously (stronger than マジ) | 2→3 | ガチで美味しい = seriously delicious. |
| ばり | bari | very / insanely | 3 | Kansai/Hakata youth slang. ばりうま = insanely good. |
| 鬼 | oni | extremely (lit. "demon") | 3 | 鬼うまい = demon-level tasty. |

## Notes for Phrase Curation

- All Gen Z (level 3) terms should be tested with native speakers 25-35 before inclusion.
- Some terms are strongly gendered (すげえ = masculine, あげぽよ = feminine).
- Internet slang (草, ぴえん) is primarily text — may sound odd spoken aloud.
- Kansai-origin terms (めっちゃ, ばり, それな) are now nationwide but carry regional flavor.
- The 〇〇る pattern is productive — new verbs appear constantly.
