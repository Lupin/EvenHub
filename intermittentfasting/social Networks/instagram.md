## Post 3 — Why Glasses (June 20)

**Type:** Single image

**Visual:** Side-by-side — phone with fasting app (dimmed) vs glasses with display visible

**Copy:**
> I used to track fasts on my phone. Open the app, check the timer, close it. Repeat a few times a day. It works, but it's friction.
>
> With the G2, the information is just there. Glance down, see where you are, keep going. It sounds minor but after a week it changes how you experience a fast — less checking, more awareness.
>
> That's the whole idea behind this app. Not a phone replacement, just a different place to put the information.
>
> #IntermittentFasting #EvenRealities

## Post 4 — How It Works (June 22)

**Type:** Single image or text-overlay

**Visual:** Dark background with app UI elements arranged

**Copy:**
> A few details about how Intermittent Fasting works on G2:
>
> Everything stays on your device. No account, no cloud sync, no analytics. The config is saved in localStorage and that's it.
>
> The companion app (on your phone) is where you pick a preset and set your schedule. After that, the glasses display updates on its own — the phone doesn't need to be open.
>
> When you cross from eating to fasting (or vice versa), the glasses show a brief transition label and your phone gets a notification with a sound. Helpful if you tend to lose track of time.
>
> Available in English and French. Dark and light theme on the companion app.
>
> #IntermittentFasting #EvenRealities
